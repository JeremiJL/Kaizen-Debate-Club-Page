Facilitator notes

Compact prompts for building the policy framework and keeping the clashes concrete.

  • Start by agreeing on the minimum parts of a solid policy: problem diagnosis, mechanism, incentives, implementation, and likely side effects.
  • After each draft policy, assign one speaker to red-team it for loopholes, bad incentives, hidden costs, and enforceability problems.
  • For the religion motion, press on legitimacy, social function, pluralism, and whether meaning can be deliberately engineered.
  • For the birth-rate motion, demand clarity on root causes, coercion risks, time horizon, and why the chosen intervention beats alternatives.
  • For the university-senate motion, force specificity on curriculum design, employability, governance, and what is lost by merging the tracks.
